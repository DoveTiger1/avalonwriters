Don't make their mistake. Do this instead to get paid in this economy

By Chukwuma Chibuike

Hey there, fancy supply chain and logistics?

Ever wondered how your mail always reaches you?

How the groceries in your favorite department stores always get restocked.

And how come every company, office, factory, and just about every human activity always has the supplies they need to function properly?

I bet you have, and you probably know it's all made possible by the smooth running and efficiency of the supply chains and logistics.

So read on. I've got something to tell you.

Listen:

We are at the peak of civilization. With Artificial Intelligence everywhere, maybe Alex Proyas'movie I, Robot ,  could soon become a reality.

Well, I don't think so. Because I don't believe AI could ever have such capabilities.

But the technological advancements of today seem unstoppable. Only a doomsday apocalypse on a global scale could stop the current technological explosion.

Or a war between the US and Russia which will certainly be kind of apocalyptic.

We live in a very unique time in history.

It's never been “as possible as it is now ”for people to raise themselves from rags to riches, or at least to the upper middle

Mark you, I said “as possible as it is now ” , not “as easy ” . Because nothing worthwhile is ever easy.

The current boom in technology and easy availability of information presents us with incredible economic opportunities as never seen before.

And the benevolent culprit to thank for the grand scale of the economic possibilities available today is technological advancement .

 Advanced technology has made it easy to access accurate information about almost anything, from anywhere.

And you not only deserve to be here, but you deserve to make your life better through these opportunities.

Because technology continues to level the playing field for you, more and more.

But there are different ways for you to do it. These time-tested and proven ways:

  1. You could become a thief, a fraudster, or a scam artist. You may not be successful though. And if you are, you might end up in jail.
  2. Or you could try to be born rich. But you've already been born. So you were either already born rich and shouldn't be on this page. Or you're just like most other people and have missed that shot. You can't return to the womb.
  3. Or you could marry rich …but it's not as easy as it sounds. A frustrating nine, three weeks, and five days on a dating website would be enough to make you quit on that.
  4. Or, you could start gambling. Play the lottery every day. Spend your hard-earned money on betting, and hope you win big  someday. Just that you have little chance of winning big .  
  • As we both know for every gazillion bets, only a few win big money.
  • So you can keep spending money you don't have to pay for bets or lottery numbers. And then wish to be the one in a million who plays the one big winning bet or numbers out of all the countless gamblers doing the same …

OR

You can finally choose to work hard and gain new skills like most other successful people in history. Successful who didn't get rich through scandalous acts or by inheritance by birth or marriage.

Research shows that 9.72 out of every 10 hard-working people succeed sooner or later. As long as they follow the laid down success principles.

Isn't that incredible odds?

Lol. Ok, that was definitely a made-up statistic. Forgive my dry humor.

But the truth is that by learning new skills you make yourself employable.

And you get to compete in the job market in the right way, according to tested and true success principles.

Because by the Law of Attraction, what you echo into the Universe through your activity will naturally return to you.

And after you've succeeded, having staked your claim the right way, you can then contribute meaningfully to the upliftment of society through your work.

These lyrical bars from Jay Z in his rap song Lights, sums it up:

“I cannot help the poor

if I'm one of'em.

So I got rich and gave back,

to me that's the win-win ”

~ Jay Z - Lights.

You get the gist I believe.

But remember that only working according to the laid down success principles will serve you here.

So how do you go about this?

Well, you 've heard it all before. Really.

You are always asked to learn a new skill or trade, to take a course online or offline, here and there. Because it will help you get a nice, high-paying job in the industry of your choosing regardless of your program of study at the university.

And it doesn't matter what you studied at the university.

And that as long as you've got some formal education under your belt …you're good to go.

And that the main purpose of formal education is to help you learn how to read and write. And to teach you how to learn anything you want —on your own, and with little guidance.

Well, these are all true.

Though, “on your own ”does not mean that you get to walk into an auto mechanic workshop, strip down all the parts like a magician, and start running mechanical diagnostics. Just like that. Without any form of protracted training.

No.

It simply means that you can choose your learning path yourself and direct your learning. Successfully.

Now, you probably frowned when you read that you should learn new skills.

All this rubble rousing just for a cliché. Such simplistic advice.

I'm sorry.

Don't frown. Just hear me out first.

We live in peculiar times when opportunities abound everywhere but nobody wants to work anymore. Everyone wants to game the system.

People will rather spend all their time researching how to earn two dollars a day from an app, how to make money by clicking on emojis and taking selfies, and things like that than try to do something productive.

Productive in the sense of earning money in a manner that helps the economy. Right? No one wants to do things that help the economy grow anymore.

We all want to post selfies on Pinterest and make viral shorts on TikTok. So we can rake in views and smile to the bank.

Well, that is possible. But how many try and fail? Numerous. Countless.

But think of the numerous open job positions worldwide, and yet the countless number of unemployed people.

What does that tell you?

In the US alone, there are currently more open positions, ( ten million) , than unemployed people, (five million). According to the National bureau of employment.

But why should people be unemployed in a country like the US when the unfilled positions outnumber the unemployed people?

It's easy. Skills. Or better said, lack of skills.

There are not enough skilled people to fill those positions. Many companies find it difficult to get skilled workers to fill their open positions.

Many high-skill job openings advertised online receive few or no applications.

And some that receive numerous applications remain unfilled even after a long time.—

Because most of those applicants are neither skilled enough nor the right fit for the positions they applied for.

And while numerous unemployed or underemployed people walk around complaining about their ill luck in the labor market, only a few of them are willing to learn anything new. They usually prefer to remain unemployed than to learn a new skill.

By being here, I assume you can be convinced to stop acting like the crowd who seem to dislike investing their time and effort toward learning anything.

I hope you would love to be like those few people who value continuous learning. The few that realize that the biggest investment they can make is to continuously learn new things and develop themselves.

So it's time for you to start learning new skills. Because if you do, you will hardly go unemployed again.

Now I'm going to try and avoid hitting you with another cliché by asking you to learn programming and IT skills because the jobs are too many and pay so well.

You've probably heard that it is difficult to learn programming well enough to be good enough for commercial software development.

Well, it's true.

To start from zero and get to the stage where a global machine like a company or business will trust you with their code base, and expect you to tinker out programs that solve internal problems for them and/or serve their customers'needs, is a tall order.

It takes a lot of time. You will have sleepless and frustrating nights of seemingly no progress. And you will need incredible persistence, and some stroke of good luck to pull it off.

Software development pays extremely well, but it's not easy.

This is not to discourage you from a thrill-filled, interesting, and dynamic industry with massive importance and role for the global economy.

You can take a crack at software development. Have patience, stick with it, and be in the learning zone for the long haul. And you will certainly smile at it later.

But some industries pay equally well and have lots of opportunities for career development and progression which also lack skilled workers.

Such industries are not less important and are not even decoupled from IT.

No industry today is decoupled from IT.

And rising to executive positions in such industries does not require less skill and hard work, and does not have a lower learning curve.

No. They rather have a somewhat lower barrier of entry. You can learn a little, get a job, and start getting paid quickly. Much faster than in the IT industry or for IT positions.

Yet the potential for career progression is not lower than you have for IT positions.

Once you're in a job, you can continue to learn, develop yourself, and rise, until you get to the top.

And one of such simpler  industries with lots of open positions is the supply chain industry.

The barrier of entry for a skilled supply chain job is not that low, but with some effort, it is very doable, even without a university degree.

And very importantly, there are plenty of openings looking for people like you to fill them.

If you can do the job and deliver the results, you are needed and welcomed even if you studied library science.

So, just as for many IT positions where it does't matter what you studied, so is it here.

But the good thing about the supply chain for you is that it has a faster learning curve from total novice to getting your feet into the job industry than programming and IT.

The jobs also offer better work-life balance than the high-pressure and deadline-filled IT jobs.

And I can show you how to go about learning enough about the supply chain to get a job easily.

I'll wrap it up here with the belief that you should be interested in knowing more.
