Why More People Should Engage In Ecotourism

"ecotourism is just what the world needs to create the much-needed genuine personal appreciation of nature in people"

If you had to guess what most people would choose if they were asked to choose between:

strolling for sight-seeing across streets with great buildings as you would find in Dubai or Paris,

or strolling across landscapes of unadulterated natural beauty such as mountains or virgin forests,

what would you guess?

Well, like you might have guessed, probably from your own choice, most people would claim they'd love to go to the natural places in order to get close to nature.

They'd want to see the Grand Canyon, the Yellowstone, the Amazon, etc.

But if a real opportunity arises to go on vacation, those wishes are instantly thrown behind the bus and they're off to Dubai, London, Paris, etc.

They'd suddenly want to see the Big Apple. Well, there's only one New York, you know.

We all claim we love nature, want to conserve it, and want to get close to it and understand it.

But it's mostly lip service. Many people have very little knowledge about the earth's geography outside the localities where they work and live.

Most get stuck in the concepts they drew from high school geography which were mostly immature and incomplete then and which continue to get more muddled as they grow older.

With such ephemeral conceptions of the geographical features of our home planet, it's not difficult to imagine that there's even greater ignorance about the natural treasures of the earth, the beautiful and exotic resorts of nature, and the endangered habitats with their unique plant and animal species.

The average American for instance has hardly even visited many of the protected natural sites and geographical treasures within the US, let alone those outside the US.

OnePoll and Zipcar conducted research that revealed that a quarter of Americans had never visited famous US landmarks [1].

The research as reported by Matt Hilburn reveals that 8.2 out of 10 Americans had never been to the Grand Canyon and 7.7 out 10 had never been to the Niagara Falls.

Many had never even visited the landmarks in their own cities.

Yet most of the research participants claimed they would love to go out more, to visit both the Grand Canyon, Niagara Falls, and Yellowstone National Park.
But blamed economic difficulties and stringent workplace conditions as their main obstructions

Yet they never seized to spend a lot of money in new restaurants and coffee shops on a regular basis.

But ecotourism is just what the world needs to create the much-needed genuine personal appreciation of nature in people.

Because it would enable the pressing need for the conservation of nature to truly become a personal belief and not something people accept because it is constantly being ripped off by news reporters.

We are used to feasting our eyes on the environmentally unfriendly, “marvelous” constructions in large metropolitan areas. But a few people have also witnessed just how beautiful this earth can be without these constructions.

And they are usually the ones whose calls for the conservation of nature are genuine.

Through firsthand experiences, they understand that this earth which offers nearly eight billion of us a home can produce extraordinary beauty and deserves real protection.

Jean Holy Smithereens, for instance, described an experience during a visit to the Grand Canyon as “one of those moments that [she] saw with [her] own eyes that Nature is still the best architect ever” [2].

Many movements and organizations, both governmental and non-governmental, work towards the conservation of the earth's natural treasure sites and endangered habitats.

And many of these habitats are some of the most beautiful places to ever behold.

In a 2017 article on their website, CNN lists about 50 natural wonders of the earth worth visiting [3].

Visiting these natural wonders could awaken some form of earth conservation consciousness in tourists.

It could make them voluntarily participate in and support the activities of nature conservation organizations through public activism and patronage by donations and contributions.

Many nature resorts go a long way to protect their environments while making them attractive to tourists by offering leisure, beautiful accommodation, good food and stable electricity for tourists.

Some of these resorts offer five-star hospitality services for those looking to have the time of their lives. But they need patronage from more tourists to maintain and fund their businesses.

An example is the Sense Resort Khao Kho, a five-star hospitality resort located in one of Thailand's mountainous marvels.

Their five-star services combined with the glorious display of the surrounding nature's beauty would generate euphoria and ecstasy in the hearts of even the most skeptical tourists [4].

Around most natural resorts and conservation sites in rural areas, many educational and community-building activities promote the growth of the surrounding local communities.

The influx of cash tourists bring will help such local communities grow.

The tourists on the other hand could gain exposure to new cultures, languages, and art. They could also enjoy exotic delicacies native to those regions in the process.

Coupled with the natural attractions and landscapes in those places.

Tourists get to meet new and different people and could possibly build friendships that may last a lifetime.

One such example is the Feel Free Center in Gambia, based in Jinack Niji, a coastal village in the Gambia.

Aside from offering hospitality services to tourists, they also “partner closely with community leaders and villagers ... to develop sustainable environmental conservation and ecotourism projects” [5].

Among many things, they are involved in various health initiatives and aid programs and work to promote education and skills acquisition among girls.

So, it is essential to awaken the consciousness and love of nature in people. And one of the best ways to achieve this is to encourage people to patronize natural resorts and tourist attractions.

Promoting ecotourism among the populace will engender positive educational and holistic effects on the tourists themselves.

They would naturally adopt a protective stance towards the earth itself, based on their own lofty experiences and close contact with nature.

Greater ecotourism will also boost the economy of local and rural regions that offer natural attractions.

And the indigenes of these rural areas will grow through exposure to visitors from different parts of the world.
