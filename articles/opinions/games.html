Why computer games could be good for your kids

by Chukwuma Chibuike

Throughout the ages, humankind has always been curious. And this curiosity coupled with necessity forced the development of tools to aid in work and production. Science and technology were thereby developed with an emphasis on utility — to make work and life easier.

But through various stages of advancement, modern technology appeared in the scene with a rapid explosion. And all of a sudden, utility was no longer the motive behind all work. Even entertainment, fun, and leisure now receive considerable investment in human resources and skills.

Video games as a form of entertainment are a product of this modernity. But one may rightly argue that the value of these games goes beyond entertainment and that they are anything but harmful as some very conservative people wrongly believe. In fact, video games are useful to children in many ways.

First, let's quickly do away with the idea that video games are harmful. Video game addicts do not prove anything here at all. Most of them are simply good people who need a little help.

And people have been, and many still remain addicted to food, alcohol, smoking, sex, pornography, masturbation, gambling, partying, and the nightlife, watching movies, TV series, or shows of all kinds, to name many.

Smoking, and belief systems aside, someone's excessive attachment to any of the activities listed above does not make those activities inherently bad in themselves; although, one with a moralistic tendency would also add pornography and masturbation to their book of inherently bad activities. And we fully acknowledge their rights to do so even if we are not here to preach morality.

So, for an addict, what's bad is the person's predilection towards something, resulting from weakness of will and character. And having personally struggled with addictions myself, I believe I can attest to this fact.

Granted, there are violent and bad video games just as there are violent and bad movies. But just as parents can show discretion and care to control the movies their kids watch, they should also be able to do so with the games they allow their kids to play.

It is well known that some excessive gamers lose out on healthy social or even professional lives due to excessive gaming, but anything in life will always have those who exaggerate it.

Therefore if we declare people to be addicted when they are incapable of abstaining from a particular kind of activity, even for a while, to the extent that it causes harmful effects on other aspects of their lives, then it can be argued correctly that a workaholic who ends up losing his marriage due to his overly attachment to his job can also be described as being addicted to his job, regardless of his professional successes.

After all, he lost a family because he overly prioritized his profession and was unable to find a good work-life balance. Even though society would hardly frown at him, it could never really count as a good thing for him.

So the point being made here is simply that people lose their heads and therefore their balance in life over many different kinds of things, even seemingly good stuff, like a job! So, it's not just about bad activities or even the normally harmless video games which is the least of society's worries.

Now, having debunked the myth that video games might be harmful, let's come to some of the numerous benefits of playing video games.

  1. Better brains and improved hand-eye coordination

    Fast-paced games require quick reaction times. Therefore, those who play such games often develop much better coordination skills.

    Their hands and fingers act generally faster in response to an impulse or wish.

    They learn to think critically using different parts of their brains, make quick split-second decisions, apply the decisions immediately without much pondering, and see the results in an instant.

    This carries over into other domains of activities where gamers have been identified to react to questions and situations up to 25% faster than non-gamers [1].

  2. Improved feelings and emotional health

    While playing video games, gamers experience a positive adrenaline rush, a greater flow of dopamine, and feel-good hormones in the brain, the nervous system, and the entire body in general.

    Gaming has even been touted as therapeutic, giving people greater confidence, energy, and emotional positivity, and reducing stress, depression, and anxiety [1].

  3. They can also be educational

    There are many educational video games out there that are as interesting as the seemingly non-educational ones.

    And some of these help students learn using audio-visual aids, in a more hands-on and involved manner, guiding them through interesting adventures that improve their concentration and focus, and helping them to understand and absorb content much better through the concrete applications available in such games.

    By playing these games, students could develop new skills and understand schoolwork better by putting different parts of their brains to work. And this could possibly lead to improved performance and scores in school.

  4. Games help children make friends and improve their social lives

    Video games help kids in social settings by giving them what to talk about among their peers. They help defuse tension and awkward situations among new friends, giving them common ground.

    Being good in a game could make a kid look cool and acceptable among friends in the right way.

    Many times, when acquaintances and friends meet together, they play games to keep things interesting. People even set out and meet each other for the first time solely for the purpose of playing games.

    Playing games could help improve the social lives of kids through meeting new people both in person and online [2]. And create the opportunity to forge lasting and meaningful friendships and relationships.

    Multiplayer games teach kids teamwork, cooperation, and effective communication which carry through from social settings into their adulthood and even professional lives.

  5. Games could help parents teach kids discipline

    Parents can control the games their kids play and the amount of time kids spend playing them. In fact, games could be used to teach kids responsibility, accountability, and discipline.

    Kids can be encouraged to work hard by using very entertaining games as a reward for jobs well done and by withholding access when they fail to meet expectations.

    Also, by setting a limit on how much time kids spend on the games they love, kids learn that sometimes, they have to work on their studies or do chores and other activities that they may not gladly subscribe to, such as tidying up and going to bed early, or attending family social gatherings, going to the church or to the dentist.

    Because not every time should be fun time.

    So, having access to what they enjoy and leaving it behind to perform not-so-fun but necessary activities is a tool for instilling discipline in kids at an early age.

Many positive results can be achieved by using video games in different ways and for different purposes.

But one underlying experience remains among all regular players of the various games out there. Regardless of the intent or purpose, whether to kill time, fight boredom, or entertain oneself, video games are very fun to play. They are enjoyable.

And all one needs is to find what one enjoys, and in an instant, it is very easy to make oneself happy, alone, or in groups, while enjoying many side benefits.

Even adults should play. The benefits are for everyone!
